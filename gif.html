FUNNY FACTS ABOUT GIRAFFES





  • Giraffes have long necks to reach the leaves of tall trees, but did you know they only have seven neck vertebrae, just like humans?
    •

  • Despite their towering height, giraffes have relatively small hearts, about the size of a basketball, to pump blood up their long necks.


  • Giraffes have unique spot patterns, like human fingerprints, making each one easily distinguishable
    •

  • These gentle giants can run at speeds of up to 35 miles per hour, making them surprisingly fast runners.
    •

  • Giraffes often engage in a behavior known as "necking," which is not romantic but involves two males playfully fighting for dominance.
    •

  • These graceful creatures have a natural "built-in" sunscreen. Their tongues are dark-colored to prevent sunburn while feeding on leaves.
    •

  • Giraffes are silent animals and communicate mostly through body language, such as head movements and postures.


  • At birth, baby giraffes drop nearly 6 feet to the ground, which helps to break their amniotic sac and stimulate breathing.
    •

  • Giraffes have a distinctive loping gait, moving both legs on one side of their body simultaneously.
    •

  • Surprisingly, giraffes only need about 30 minutes to 2 hours of sleep per day, often taking short naps while standing.
