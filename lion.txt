1. Lions are the only social big cats, living in groups called prides.
2. Their roar can be heard up to 5 miles away.
3. Lions can run up to speeds of 50 miles per hour.
4. They spend about 16 to 20 hours a day sleeping or resting.
5. Lions are excellent swimmers and are not afraid of water.
6. Male lions have manes, which can range in color from light blonde to black.
7. A male lion's mane can indicate his age and health, as well as intimidate rival males.
8. Lions are known for their impressive hunting skills, although the majority of their hunts are unsuccessful.
9. Cubs start practicing their hunting skills as young as 2-3 months old.
10. A group of lionesses can take down large prey, such as zebras or buffalo, by working together.
11. Lions have a specialized spine that allows them to stretch and bend extremely well.
12. They communicate through various vocalizations, such as growls, meows, roars, and purrs.
13. Lions have scent glands on their paws, which help them mark their territory.
14. A lion's bite force is incredibly strong, capable of exerting pressures up to 600 pounds per square inch.
15. Lions can consume up to 40 pounds of meat in a single meal.
16. They have a keen sense of hearing and can hear prey from great distances.
17. In a pride, females do the majority of the hunting while males protect the territory.
18. Male lions often sleep up to 20 hours a day.
19. Lions have been featured in many ancient cultures, like the Egyptians and Greeks, as symbols of strength and royalty.
20. Lions have been known to form strong bonds with their pride members, engaging in social grooming and affectionate behaviors.
